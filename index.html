Molly Riley

Ed.S. | Child, Family, and School Psychology

Molly obtained her Education Specialist Degree in Child, Family, and School Psychology at the University Denver and her Bachelor of Science degree in Experimental Psychology from the University of South Carolina. Molly’s career is grounded in her belief in the impact of a powerful mindset alongside her passion for empowering clients to practice self-discovery and self-awareness. Molly has worked in private, clinical, and educational settings. She has a wide range of experience working with school aged children to young adults both in person and virtually. Through the virtual setting, Molly is able to work with her clients in the comfort of their own home or any space they choose. Her clients are able to participate from the space where they feel safest. This also allows for flexibility with families who may have a busy schedule or may not have consistent access to transportation. While virtual therapy can be a wonderful fit for anyone, she's found that it is especially fit for clients with anxiety, depression, and those who struggle with social interactions and relationship building. She believes in taking a whole-person approach with each individual and considering the many factors of a person’s self that may contribute to their life experience. Outside of her work in psychology, Molly enjoys traveling, snowboarding, and attempting to keep up with her golden retriever.

What is our process?

Let's start the conversation to see if we are the right fit for your needs.

Step 1: Contact Us

The first step is for you to fill out the Appointment Request Form below. We will respond with an email or phone call and together we can determine whether or not to move forward with scheduling a consultation appointment.

Step 2: Complete Forms

After deciding to move forward, you will submit an intake form. After submission, we will email you an invitation to join Therapy Appointment where you will complete the required new patient documentation.

Step 3: Initial Appointment

After completing the new patient documentation, we will contact you to set up a consultation appointment. This appointment is how we determine the best therapy or treatment plan for you. Once a patient, you will use Therapy Appointment to book future appointments.
